**For a developer, MVP means *Minimum Viable Product*—the simplest working version of a product that delivers core value to users, lets you test assumptions, and gather feedback before investing in full-scale development.** It’s not just a prototype; it must be usable, valuable, and viable enough for real users to interact with.  [GeeksForGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/software-engineering/mvp-in-software-development-a-complete-overview/)  [appverticals.com](https://www.appverticals.com/blog/mvp-in-software-development/)  [Atlassian](https://www.atlassian.com/agile/product-management/minimum-viable-product)  

---

## 🔑 What MVP Means in Software Development
- **Minimum**: Only essential features that prove the product’s core value proposition.  
- **Viable**: Must genuinely work for users—no broken demos or incomplete flows.  
- **Product**: Usable enough that people can try it, understand it, and get value.  [appverticals.com](https://www.appverticals.com/blog/mvp-in-software-development/)  

---

## 🎯 Why Developers Build MVPs
- **Validate ideas quickly**: Test if the market cares before investing heavily.  
- **Gather feedback**: Early adopters provide insights to refine features.  
- **Reduce risk**: Avoid wasting resources on products with no demand.  
- **Cost-effective**: Build lean, iterate based on data, not assumptions.  [GeeksForGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/software-engineering/mvp-in-software-development-a-complete-overview/)  

---

## 📊 MVP vs Other Early-Stage Builds

| Term | Purpose | Key Difference |
|------|----------|----------------|
| **Prototype** | Visual/mock-up of product | Not functional, used to explore design ideas |
| **PoC (Proof of Concept)** | Tests technical feasibility | Focuses on whether something *can* be built |
| **MVP** | Functional product with core features | Focuses on whether users *want* it and will use it |  [GeeksForGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/software-engineering/mvp-in-software-development-a-complete-overview/)  

---

## ⚡ Examples of MVPs
- **Amazon (early days)**: Started as a simple online bookstore before expanding into other categories.  
- **Dropbox**: Launched with a demo video showing core functionality before building full infrastructure.  
- **Airbnb**: Began with a simple website renting out the founders’ apartment to test demand.  [Atlassian](https://www.atlassian.com/agile/product-management/minimum-viable-product)  

---

## 🚀 Developer Takeaways
- **Focus on the “must-have” features** that validate your product’s problem-solution fit.  
- **Don’t confuse “minimum” with “incomplete”**—users must still get real value.  
- **Iterate fast**: Release, learn from feedback, improve.  
- **Use MVP as a decision tool**: Scale if demand exists, pivot if feedback suggests changes, or kill the idea if no traction.  [appverticals.com](https://www.appverticals.com/blog/mvp-in-software-development/)
